Substance

So much of today's web is bite-size, ephemeral, and disposable. We love the web. But when it comes to our local communities, we need a different kind of website — one that values longevity as well as content.

Residents of Santa Cruz, California, are sharing and cataloging the local area's incredible natural beauty. Folks in the Triangle Region in North Carolina are preserving local history by compiling pages about historic places.

Context

With user editable maps, every page has a real sense of place. And every page contributes to a rich, interlinked local resource that gets better and better over time.

In San Francisco, residents can now more easily talk about supervisorial districts because they have mapped them all out using LocalWiki. In the Triangle Region, residents are mapping the local greenways and public parks.

Character

Local communities should be free to fully express themselves on the web. That's why every LocalWiki project reflects the unique personality and perspective of its community.

Santa Cruzans document the bizzare and wonderful things that make Santa Cruz what it is. Dentonites discuss the awesome local music scene.
